Travel to Malaysia

Malaysia is a Southeast Asian country comprised of 13 states and three federal territories, divided by the South China Sea into Peninsular Malaysia and Borneo's East Malaysia. It is a federal parliamentary constitutional monarchy, with a population of over 33 million (2025 est.) and a multi-ethnic society, primarily composed of Malay, Chinese, and Indian communities.

Petronas Twin Towers

Known for their Islamic-inspired, stainless steel and glass design, they remain the world's tallest twin towers, connected by a 58.4-meter double-decker skybridge on floors 41 and 42.

KL Tower

The Kuala Lumpur Tower (KL Tower), or Menara Kuala Lumpur, is a 421-meter (1,381 ft) telecommunications tower in Malaysia, making it the seventh tallest in the world and the tallest in Southeast Asia. Completed in 1996, it offers panoramic 360-degree city views from its 276-meter observation deck, a sky deck at 300 meters, a glass-floored Sky Box, and a revolving restaurant.

Sky Bridge

The Langkawi SkyBridge is a 125-meter-long curved pedestrian suspension bridge located 700 meters above sea level on Gunung Mat Chincang in Langkawi, Malaysia. Opened in 2005, it is one of the world's longest curved bridges, offering 360-degree views of the Andaman Sea and surrounding islands. Access is via the Langkawi Cable Car.
